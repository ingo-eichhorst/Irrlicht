## Approaches

- **baseline** — current production model: pure observed round-rate, no
  estimate until the first round completes (the control).
- **prior-bootstrap** — show `total_rounds × prior` at zero rounds, then the
  pure observed rate once a round lands (identical to baseline thereafter).
- **prior-blend** — Bayesian shrinkage toward the prior at every turn,
  `(w·prior + n·observed)/(w+n)`.
- **ewma** — exponentially-weighted per-round duration over the marker
  history (stateful; shippable only by widening the forecast seam).
- **token-aware** — considered and rejected: with no token *budget* target,
  remaining-time-from-tokens reduces algebraically to the elapsed round-rate
  (`remainingTokens × secsPerToken = (elapsed/completed) × remainingRounds`),
  so it adds no signal beyond rounds for *completion-time* prediction.

## Recommendation

Ship **prior-bootstrap**.

- **Surfaces sooner (the issue's primary goal):** a number appears in 0s vs baseline's 83s, at 100% episode coverage vs 100%.
- **Provably no accuracy regression:** after the first round, prior-bootstrap runs baseline's exact code path, so its prediction is identical — the `baseline` row *is* prior-bootstrap on measured turns (MAE 21.0m, median 3.1m). Its own MAE 20.6m / median 4.7m additionally fold in the new zero-round turns it predicts where baseline showed nothing.
- **prior-blend — measured-better-MAE, not shipped:** shrinking the rate toward the prior at every turn cuts MAE to 16.8m (best swept) but raises the median to 5.1m and changes the number on *every* session, not just the zero-round gap — a larger blast radius for a mean-only win. Left as a documented follow-up.
- The zero-round number is shown with a deliberately wide (≈2×) range to signal a population prior, not a measured rate.
